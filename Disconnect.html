// THE STORY

The Story

An adult man returns to a game he obsessively played as a teenager. As he explores its familiar worlds, memories begin resurfacing — slowly at first, then all at once.

What first appears to be nostalgia slowly becomes something much darker. The game world and reality begin colliding, revealing pieces of a childhood he buried so deeply that he can no longer remember why he forgot.

Players must reconstruct the truth hidden between two realities.

  • What happened
  • How it felt
  • Neither world tells the complete story

// GAME SYSTEM

Dissociation

The longer the player remains inside the Escape World, the more reality begins to deteriorate. Visual and audio systems degrade together — the game's own interface becomes unreliable.

The player must balance comfort and truth. Staying too long means losing grip on both worlds.

// ANTAGONIST

The One Who Wants You To Forget

// ENTITY UNCLASSIFIED — DESIGNATION: UNKNOWN_

The game's primary antagonist is not a monster. It is a distorted version of the protagonist himself — created by years of suppression and avoidance — existing to keep painful memories buried.

In the Escape World, it can be fought. In reality, it can only be escaped.

As the story progresses, the line between hunter and protector becomes increasingly unclear.
